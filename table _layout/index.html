1 rani ki vav:

Address: Mohan Nagar Society, Mohan Nagar Socity, Patan, Gujarat 384265
UNESCO Site Id: 922
Area: 4.68 ha (11.6 acres)
Inscription: 2014 (38th Session)

Rani Ki Vav is a stepwell situated in the town of Patan in Gujarat, India. It is located on the banks of the Saraswati River. Its construction is attributed to Udayamati, the spouse of the 11th-century Chaulukya king Bhima I. Silted over, it was rediscovered in the 1940s and restored in the 1980s by the Archaeological Survey of India. .

2 The Ajanta cave:

Address: Ajanta, Aurangabad district, Maharashtra, India
Area: 8,242 ha
Inscription: 1983 (7th Session)

The Ajanta Caves are approximately thirty rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments dating from the second century BCE to about 480 CE in the Chatrapati Sambhajinagar district of Maharashtra state in India. The caves include paintings and rock-cut sculptures described as among the finest surviving examples of ancient Indian art.

4 Khajuraho Group of Monuments

Address: Chhatarpur, Madhya Pradesh, India
Reference: 240
Inscription: 1986 (10th Session)

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments are a group of Hindu and Jain temples in Chhatarpur district, Madhya Pradesh, India. They are about 175 kilometers southeast of Jhansi, 10km from Azad Nagar Khajwa, 9km from Rajnagar and 49 km from district headquarter Chhatarpur. They are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
